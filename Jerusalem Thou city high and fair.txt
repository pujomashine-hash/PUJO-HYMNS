JERUSALEM, THOU CITY HIGH AND FAIR
Composer: Johann Matthäus Meyfart (1626)
Translation: Catherine Winkworth (1858)

1. Jerusalem, thou city high and fair,
   Would God I were in thee!
   My longing heart fain, fain would be in there,
   It will not stay with me;
   Far over stone and mountain,
   Far over field and plain,
   It hastes to seek its Fountain
   And quit this world of pain.

2. O happy day, and yet far happier hour,
   When wilt thou come at last?
   When fearless to my Father’s love and pow’r,
   Whose promise standeth fast,
   My soul I shall surrender,
   To Him who gave it mine,
   And see with glory tender
   His face within me shine.

3. O what the joy, O what the glory be,
   When I at last shall stand
   Within the gates of pearl and crystal sea,
   In that celestial land!
   Where saints in light adore Him,
   And angels strike the lyre,
   And cast their crowns before Him,
   Amid the heavenly choir.

4. Unnumbered choirs before the shining throne
   Their joyful anthems raise,
   Till Heaven’s glad halls are echoing with the tone
   Of perfect love and praise.
   One holy song uniting,
   Through all the realms above,
   In God’s own face delighting,
   And basking in His love.
