IN THE MIDST OF LIFE
Composer: Martin Luther (1524)
Translation: Catherine Winkworth (1862)

1. In the midst of life, behold,
   Death has girt us round.
   Whom for help shall we behold,
   Where shall rest be found?
   In Thee, Lord, Thee only.
   For our sins we justly die,
   Yet, O Lord, to Thee we cry:
   Holy Lord and God,
   Holy and mighty God,
   Holy and most merciful Savior,
   Eternal God,
   Leave us not to sink in death's dark flood.
   Kyrie eleison.

2. In the midst of death's dark night,
   Hell's black gulfs expand.
   Who can save us from their might
   With a mighty hand?
   Thou, Lord, Thou only.
   Thy great pity moves Thee still
   To avert the coming ill.
   Holy Lord and God,
   Holy and mighty God,
   Holy and most merciful Savior,
   Eternal God,
   Save us from the pains of hell, O Lord.
   Kyrie eleison.

3. In the midst of utter woe
   Sins our souls oppress.
   Where shall we for refuge go
   In our deep distress?
   To Thee, Lord, Thee only.
   Who hast shed Thy precious blood
   All our guilt to purge, O God!
   Holy Lord and God,
   Holy and mighty God,
   Holy and most merciful Savior,
   Eternal God,
   In the hour of death, sustain us, Lord.
   Kyrie eleison.
